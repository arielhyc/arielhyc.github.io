Stream

Stream is a serious game focusing on teaching teenagers programming mind-set. It won 2019 G4C(Games for Change) Learn To Code Games Fellowship.

Using water flow to simulate data streaming, “filters” and “pipes” to symbolize operations and functions that will affect the outcome of the water, we hope players will associate programming concept with the visualization of our game instead of mere lines of code and inspire them to develop a programming mind-set before learning any programming languages.

My role: Concept Design & Art Design

Trashland

Trashland is a turn-based strategy board game. All of the players are survivors on the trash land, struggling on the brink of starvation and seeking for food resources. They need to spend action points to move and shoot off trash, avoiding collision with other active trash. Action and think over the best placement of trash is the challenging part of the game. If collide with active trash, one will lose the game, when all the others are out of game, the winner emerges.

My role: Concept Design & Art Design
